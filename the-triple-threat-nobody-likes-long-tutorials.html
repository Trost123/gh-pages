The Triple Threat - nobody likes long tutorials

Inspired by mobile game Democracy (which, in turn was inspired by Tripple Town), I wanted to make a zombie-apocalypse themed game with same mechanics. Mostly because I felt like programming unique unit behaviours would be fun. I shared this idea with a friend and he agreed to take care of graphics and testing.

The first rule of this game is: place 3 (or more) similar units in adjacent tiles and they will be combined into a new one.

So, 3 food units = backpack. And what if we do this process 3 times to combine 3 backpacks?

How about doing these two combinations in one turn?

Every unit had an information card describing it's abilities:

 

For example, as you play the game - crawling zombies would move around the field every 4 turns (notice the filling outline):

And so they could combine themselves:

You could build a big Tank unit that would kill zombies around it:

In total there are 16 unique units, each with their special behaviors: 5 yellow (survivors), 4 blue (defenders), 5 red (zombies). Most of them were created by combining. The gameplay looked something like this:

You could use the helicopter to move a unit from one tile to another, you had a storage slot if you wanted to save some unit for later use, you had dynamite to blow up zombies and get money for that.

The game also featured 10 missions, completing which awarded you extra money, which you could spend in shop:

Each unit affected the chaos\order ballance (red\blue vertical bar), which made you profit more\less from all sources. The goal of the game was to get 100% vaccine development (for a zombe virus). You could do so by building a Curemobiles (level 6 yellow unit), which generated a small % every few turs and by boosting development by 5% for $5000 in shop. 

You lose if you don't have any legit moves. Gameover check required me to check 4 different conditions (is the field filled? can the player use dynamite\helicopter? can he buy one in shop? is his storage slot empty?).

Programming and testing this from ground-up was fun. A lot of testing, a lot of funny bugs and a feeling of acomplishment when everything worked.

In fact, I opened the game to make a few screenshots for this text and spent an hour playing because it's quite addictive.

Now we get to the sad part: there wasn't a lot of people who figured out how to play this game, becasue we were too lazy to develop a proper tutorial.
There were too many things to explain and our tutorial ended up being too long and overloaded with text.

Nobody wants to read a 4-page rulebook before playing a flash game.

You could try though. Play The Triple Thread online here.
